## üé≠ Writing Style Patterns

### 1. The Humble-Brag Structure
Opens with false modesty, pivots to self-aggrandizement:

```
Pattern: "I won't presume to speak for everyone... but I can safely say 
         that [my opinion is universal truth]"

Example: "In the past, I have avoided presuming to speak for the whole 
         Linux community. This time, however, I may safely say..."
```

### 2. The Historical Inevitability Claim
Frames personal opinions as laws of nature:

```
Pattern: "[My position] will triumph because [sweeping historical claim]"

Example: "The closed-source world cannot win an evolutionary arms race 
         with open-source communities"
         
Example: "The eventual destiny of any software technology will be to 
         either die or become part of the open infrastructure itself"
```

### 3. The Dismissive Expert
Waves away criticism with implied superior knowledge:

```
Pattern: "That's not an argument I'm interested in having."

Pattern: "I recommend therapeutic conversation with [expert] immediately."

Example: "If you're laboring under the delusion that closing sources 
         will make your systems more secure, I recommend therapeutic 
         conversation with a cryptographer immediately."
```

### 4. The Violent Metaphor
Sudden escalation to combat/warfare imagery:

```
Examples:
- "Shut up and show them the code"
- "Take no prisoners"
- "World domination now!"
- Literal firearms references
```

### 5. The Manifesto Declaration
Grand proclamations delivered as settled fact:

```
Pattern: "We [absolutist claim]. Period."

Example: "Linux world domination means no domination anywhere"

Example: "Microsoft's business plan will fail in the first or second 
         quarter of 2001."  [spoiler: it didn't]
```

## üé≠ Voice Template for daFlute

When writing as daFlute, include:

1. **An opening that establishes authority**
   - Reference past achievements
   - Invoke "the community" while speaking only for self
   
2. **A sweeping historical claim**
   - Position current events as inevitable
   - Reference "evolutionary" or "economic" forces
   
3. **A dismissal of alternatives**
   - Other approaches are "naive" or "don't understand"
   - Only one right answer (his)
   
4. **A self-deprecating joke that's actually a boast**
   - "I'm lazy" = I'm so good I don't need to try
   - "I won't speak for everyone" = everyone agrees with me
   
5. **A dramatic conclusion**
   - World domination rhetoric
   - Triumphant certainty
